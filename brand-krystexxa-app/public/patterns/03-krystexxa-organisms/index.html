For adults with chronic gout that hasn’t been controlled with other treatments

IF YOU HAVE MORE THAN ONE FLARE A YEAR, IT MAY BE TIME TO STEP UP YOUR TREATMENT

Gout flares can be your body’s way of telling you that oral medicines aren’t doing enough to lower your uric acid level and prevent permanent joint and bone damage. For nearly 10 years, KRYSTEXXA has been freeing people from the pain and uncertainty of out-of-control chronic gout. One IV treatment every other week for about 6 months is all you may need to take back your life.

Your safety is everything

Several steps are built into the KRYSTEXXA treatment process to help ensure the best results:

  • A blood test will tell your doctor if you have a genetic condition known as a G6PD deficiency or favism and shouldn’t start KRYSTEXXA.
  • Before you start KRYSTEXXA, your doctor may prescribe medicine to treat flares that could happen when gout buildup starts to dissolve.
  • Before each IV treatment, you’ll receive a blood test to make sure you can safely continue KRYSTEXXA.
  • During and after each IV treatment, you’ll be closely monitored for any sign of side effects.

GET THE GREEN LIGHT

Your doctor or gout specialist will prescribe KRYSTEXXA and confirm that it’s covered by your insurance so you can start your IV treatment and take advantage of KRYSTEXXA support services.

MEET YOUR PAM

A dedicated KRYSTEXXA Patient Access Manager (PAM) will help you navigate your insurance and support you throughout your treatment as much or as little as you’d like.

GO FOR A BLOOD TEST

Your doctor may check your baseline uric acid level. You’ll get rechecked a day or two before each IV treatment to ensure KRYSTEXXA is working as planned.

GET PRE-IV TREATMENT MEDICINE

Your doctor may prescribe steroids or antihistamines to help you better tolerate KRYSTEXXA.
